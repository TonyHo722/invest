Anomaly Report

Delisted Stock Screening Anomaly

Ticker Focus: 8303.T (SBI Shinsei Bank) • Resolved: 2026-06-27

🔍 The Issue Overview

During the screening of the Japanese JPX Prime Market, 8303.T (SBI Shinsei Bank) was flagged as an attractive investment candidate matching the criteria for being significantly below its 200-Day Moving Average (200-DMA).

However, SBI Shinsei Bank was officially delisted from the Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE) on September 28, 2023, following a privatization transaction by SBI Holdings.

⚠️ Why was it still returning price data? Third-party data providers and platforms like Yahoo Finance often publish fixed settlement values (e.g. privatization bid levels near 1,380 - 1,400 JPY) as daily updates. This creates the illusion of active trading records when fetched programmatically.

📉 The Root Cause Analysis

The root cause of this anomaly lies in the unadjusted split data provided by Yahoo Finance's historical price feeds:

Metric Type Value Explanation
Last Close Price 1,389.5 JPY The fixed settlement/privatization closing price.
Unadjusted 200-DMA 3,238,175,242.88 JPY Distorted by an unadjusted 1-for-20,000,000 reverse split on September 27, 2023.
% Difference -99.999957% The calculated difference between the close price and the distorted 200-DMA.

Because the historic price records prior to the split were multiplied by 20,000,000, the 200-DMA grew to a multi-billion JPY figure, creating a massive artificial price drop that bypassed normal screener logic.

🛠️ Technical Resolution

To prevent delisted or highly distorted companies from appearing in active reports, we implemented a dual-layered protection mechanism in scripts/screener.py:

Layer 1: Percentage Difference Anomaly Filter (Dynamic)

We filter out any candidates that are extremely far below their 200-DMA (e.g., >80% below the DMA), which is statistically impossible under normal market conditions and always points to split errors or delisting settlements.

Layer 2: Hardcoded Blacklist (Pre-emptive)

We maintain an explicit blacklist of known delisted tickers to filter them out instantly at the start of the execution, saving network requests and caching overhead.
